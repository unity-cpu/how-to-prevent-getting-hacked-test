Top 5 things that prevent most hacks

  1. Use a password manager and make every password unique.
  2. Turn on MFA/2FA (prefer authenticator app) for email, banking, socials.
  3. Update your OS, browser, and apps automatically.
  4. Think before you click—verify unexpected links/attachments.
  5. Back up important files to the cloud and an external drive.

These five habits block the majority of common attacks like credential stuffing, phishing, and ransomware.

How Hacks Happen

How Hacks Happen

Hacks often begin with phishing emails, weak passwords, or outdated software that attackers exploit. Social engineering techniques trick users into giving away access without realizing it.

Malware Types

Common Malware Types

  • Viruses: Infect and spread through files or systems.
  • Worms: Self-replicate and travel across networks without needing help.
  • Ransomware: Locks your data and demands payment to unlock it.
  • Spyware: Secretly monitors activity and steals data.
  • Trojans: Pretend to be harmless but actually open a way for attackers.
Protect Yourself

How to Protect Yourself

  • Use strong, unique passwords with a password manager
  • Turn on two-factor authentication (2FA)
  • Keep your device and apps up to date
  • Avoid clicking on suspicious links or attachments
  • Back up your important files regularly
Spot Phishing

How to Spot Phishing

  • Sender Is Off: Check the sender’s email carefully.
  • Urgent Messages: Phishing attempts often create panic.
  • Typos & Bad Grammar: Real businesses typically proofread thoroughly.
  • Strange Links: Hover to preview link URLs before clicking.
  • Requests for Sensitive Info: Legitimate companies don’t ask for your password via email.
If You’re Hacked

Steps to Take if You’re Hacked

  1. Change Important Passwords: Start with email, banking, social media.
  2. Enable 2FA: If it was off, turn it on immediately.
  3. Run a Malware Scan: Use antivirus tools to clean your system.
  4. Warn Your Contacts: Tell friends or colleagues if your account might be compromised.
  5. Report & Notify: Report to the service provider and relevant authorities if needed.
